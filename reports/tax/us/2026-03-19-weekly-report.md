### 2. Digital Services Tax at Federal and State Level
- **Jurisdiction: Federal**
  - **What Changed/Proposed**: No federal digital services tax (DST) exists as of 2023. The U.S. has resisted international DST frameworks (e.g., OECD Pillar 1) due to concerns over double taxation, as per U.S. Treasury statements in 2023.
  - **Effective Date/Status**: Not enacted; ongoing international negotiations.
  - **Impact on Invoica**: No immediate federal DST liability, but future adoption of OECD rules could impose taxes on digital revenue, affecting platform fees.
  - **Source**: U.S. Treasury Press Releases (treasury.gov).

- **Jurisdiction: Maryland (State-Level DST)**
  - **What Changed/Proposed**: Maryland’s Digital Advertising Gross Revenues Tax, enacted in 2021, imposes a tax on digital advertising revenue for companies with global annual gross revenues over $100 million and Maryland digital ad revenue over $1 million. Rates range from 2.5% to 10% based on global revenue.
  - **Effective Date/Status**: Effective for tax years beginning after December 31, 2020; upheld by Maryland courts in 2023 despite legal challenges.
  - **Impact on Invoica**: If Invoica generates revenue from digital advertising in Maryland, it could be liable for this tax if thresholds are met, though payment processing alone may not trigger it.
  - **Source**: Maryland Comptroller’s Office (marylandtaxes.gov).

- **Jurisdiction: New York, California, Texas, Florida, Washington, Illinois, Massachusetts, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Colorado**
  - **What Changed/Proposed**: New York proposed a digital advertising tax in 2023 (S.1974), but it has not been enacted. Other states like California and Washington have discussed similar DSTs but lack legislation as of late 2023. Texas, Florida, Illinois, Massachusetts, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, and Colorado have no active DST proposals.
  - **Effective Date/Status**: New York proposal pending; others not enacted.
  - **Impact on Invoica**: Minimal current impact, but future state DSTs could tax digital revenue streams if Invoica expands into advertising or other taxable digital services.
  - **Source**: New York State Legislature (nysenate.gov); state tax authority websites.
