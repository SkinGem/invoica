- **What Changed/Proposed**: There are no specific federal regulations directly addressing AI agent transactions or autonomous digital commerce as of October 2023. However, the IRS treats transactions facilitated by AI agents as taxable events if they result in income or capital gains, following general tax principles under the Internal Revenue Code (IRC). The IRS has signaled interest in emerging technologies through its 2023-2024 Priority Guidance Plan, which includes potential guidance on digital transactions (not specific to AI).
- **Effective Date/Status**: No specific guidance issued; ongoing monitoring by IRS as part of broader digital economy taxation efforts.

### 2. Digital Services Tax at Federal and State Levels
- **Jurisdiction**: Federal
- **What Changed/Proposed**: No federal digital services tax (DST) exists as of October 2023. However, there have been discussions in Congress about adopting a DST similar to those in Europe, targeting large tech companies with digital revenue. No legislation has been passed, but the idea remains under review in response to OECD Pillar 1 and 2 agreements on global taxation.
- **Effective Date/Status**: Proposed; no enactment.
- **Impact on Invoica**: If enacted, a federal DST could apply to Invoica if it meets revenue thresholds (typically aimed at large platforms), imposing a tax on digital transaction revenue.
- **Source**: Congressional Research Service reports (congress.gov)
