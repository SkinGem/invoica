### 1. AI Agent Transactions and Autonomous Digital Commerce
- **Jurisdiction**: Federal (IRS)
- **What Changed/Proposed**: The IRS has not issued specific guidance on AI agent transactions or autonomous digital commerce as of 2023. However, general rules for automated transactions apply, treating AI agents as extensions of the principal (e.g., Invoica). Income generated through AI agents is taxable as business income under IRC Section 61, and expenses must be substantiated under IRC Section 162.
- **Effective Date/Status**: Ongoing application of existing tax code; no specific AI-focused guidance as of 2023.
- **Impact on Invoica**: Invoica must report income from AI agent transactions as gross income and maintain records of automated transactions for audit purposes. Without specific guidance, there’s a risk of misclassification of income or expenses if AI agents are deemed separate entities.
- **Source**: IRS.gov (General Income Tax Principles)

### 2. Digital Services Tax (Federal and State Level)
- **Jurisdiction**: Federal
- **What Changed/Proposed**: No federal digital services tax (DST) exists as of 2023. The U.S. has resisted DST proposals internationally (e.g., OECD Pillar 1) and instead focuses on trade agreements to prevent discriminatory taxation of U.S. tech companies.
- **Effective Date/Status**: Not applicable.
- **Impact on Invoica**: No direct federal DST impact, but international clients using Invoica may face foreign DSTs, affecting pricing or transaction volume.
- **Source**: IRS.gov; U.S. Treasury statements

- **Jurisdiction**: California (CA)
- **What Changed/Proposed**: No specific DST as of 2023; however, CA imposes sales tax on digital goods and services under existing law (Cal. Rev. & Tax. Code § 6201).
- **Effective Date/Status**: Ongoing.
- **Impact on Invoica**: If Invoica’s services are deemed taxable digital services in CA, it must collect sales tax from CA customers.
- **Source**: California Department of Tax and Fee Administration (CDTFA)

- **Jurisdiction**: New York (NY)
- **What Changed/Proposed**: No DST enacted as of 2023, but proposals for taxing digital advertising and data services have been discussed in state budgets (e.g., 2021-2022 budget proposals).
- **Effective Date/Status**: Not enacted; proposals pending.
- **Impact on Invoica**: Potential future tax on digital platforms if NY enacts a DST, increasing costs for NY-based transactions.
- **Source**: NY State Department of Taxation and Finance

- **Jurisdiction**: Texas (TX), Florida (FL), Washington (WA), Illinois (IL), Massachusetts (MA), New Jersey (NJ), Pennsylvania (PA), Colorado (CO)
- **What Changed/Proposed**: WA imposes a 1.5% DST on digital advertising for businesses with global revenue over $1 billion (effective 2020, WA HB 1783). Other states have no specific DST as of 2023, though sales tax on digital services applies broadly (e.g., TX, MA).
- **Effective Date/Status**: WA DST effective since 2020; others ongoing via sales tax rules.
- **Impact on Invoica**: Minimal direct impact unless Invoica engages in digital advertising in WA or expands services taxable under state sales tax laws.
- **Source**: State tax authority websites; MTC
