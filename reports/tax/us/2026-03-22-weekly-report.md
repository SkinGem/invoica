### 1. AI Agent Transactions and Autonomous Digital Commerce
- **Jurisdiction**: Federal (IRS)
- **What Changed/Proposed**: There are no specific federal tax regulations directly addressing AI agent transactions or autonomous digital commerce as of October 2023. However, the IRS has indicated in its 2023-2024 Priority Guidance Plan (published on irs.gov) an intent to explore taxation of digital economy transactions, which could encompass AI-driven commerce. General tax principles apply, treating AI agent transactions as taxable events if they involve income generation or value transfer.
- **Effective Date/Status**: No specific guidance issued; ongoing monitoring by IRS for digital economy taxation frameworks.
- **Impact on Invoica**: Without specific guidance, Invoica must treat AI agent transactions as standard business income or payment processing under existing rules (e.g., IRC Section 61 for gross income). This means reporting income from fees or services rendered by AI agents. Lack of clarity may pose compliance risks if new rules are retroactively applied.

- **Jurisdiction**: State Level (General)
- **What Changed/Proposed**: States have not yet issued specific regulations for AI agent transactions. However, states like California and New York are exploring broader digital economy taxes (see Section 8 below) that could indirectly affect AI-driven commerce.
- **Effective Date/Status**: No enacted laws as of 2023; proposals under discussion.
- **Impact on Invoica**: Potential future state taxes on digital transactions could increase compliance burdens for AI agent payment processing.

### 2. Digital Services Tax (DST) at Federal and State Levels
- **Jurisdiction**: Federal
- **What Changed/Proposed**: No federal Digital Services Tax exists as of 2023. The U.S. has resisted international DST frameworks (e.g., OECD Pillar 1) and instead focuses on trade negotiations to prevent foreign DSTs from targeting U.S. companies. Congressional discussions (via congress.gov) indicate no imminent federal DST proposal.
- **Effective Date/Status**: Not applicable; no federal DST enacted or proposed.
- **Impact on Invoica**: No direct federal DST impact, but international exposure (if applicable) could involve foreign DST liabilities.

- **Jurisdiction**: State Level (CA, NY, TX, FL, WA, IL, MA, NJ, PA, CO)
  - **Maryland**: Enacted a Digital Advertising Services Tax (MD Tax-General § 7.5-101), effective March 14, 2021, targeting gross revenues from digital advertising for companies with over $100 million in global revenue and $1 million in Maryland revenue. Rate: 2.5% to 10% depending on revenue.
    - **Impact on Invoica**: Unlikely to apply unless Invoica engages in digital advertising services meeting revenue thresholds.
  - **New York**: Proposed Digital Advertising Tax (S.B. 1124, introduced 2023) targeting revenue from digital ads, but not yet enacted as of October 2023.
    - **Status**: Pending; no effective date.
    - **Impact on Invoica**: Potential future liability if expanded to cover broader digital services like payment processing.
  - **Other States (CA, TX, FL, WA, IL, MA, NJ, PA, CO)**: No enacted DSTs as of 2023. California and Washington have discussed DST proposals, but none are finalized (per state tax authority websites and MTC updates).
    - **Impact on Invoica**: Monitor for future DSTs that could target digital platforms or blockchain-based services.
