### 1. AI Agent Transactions and Autonomous Digital Commerce
- **Jurisdiction**: Federal (IRS)
- **What Changed/Proposed**: There are no specific federal tax regulations directly addressing AI agent transactions or autonomous digital commerce as of 2023. However, the IRS has indicated interest in emerging technologies under its broader digital asset and technology initiatives (e.g., IRS 2023-2025 Strategic Plan, available on irs.gov). General tax principles apply, treating AI-driven transactions as business income or expense based on the nature of the activity.
- **Effective Date/Status**: Ongoing monitoring; no specific regulation as of October 2023.
- **Impact on Invoica**: Without specific guidance, Invoica must classify AI agent transactions under existing tax rules (e.g., income from services under IRC Section 61). If AI agents are deemed independent contractors or entities, 1099 reporting may be required. Uncertainty in classification could lead to compliance risks.
- **Source**: IRS Strategic Plan 2023-2025 (irs.gov).

### 2. Digital Services Tax at Federal and State Level
- **Federal**:
  - **What Changed/Proposed**: No federal digital services tax (DST) exists as of 2023. Discussions in Congress (e.g., hearings on digital economy taxation) have not resulted in legislation. The U.S. Trade Representative has opposed foreign DSTs but has not proposed a domestic equivalent.
  - **Effective Date/Status**: No active proposal as of October 2023.
  - **Impact on Invoica**: No direct impact currently, but future federal DST proposals could impose taxes on digital payment processing or blockchain services.
  - **Source**: U.S. Trade Representative reports (ustr.gov); Congress.gov.
- **States** (CA, NY, TX, FL, WA, IL, MA, NJ, PA, CO):
  - **Maryland**: Enacted a Digital Advertising Gross Revenues Tax (first state DST) in 2021, effective for tax years beginning after December 31, 2020. Applies to companies with global annual gross revenues over $100 million and at least $1 million from digital advertising in Maryland. Rate: 2.5% to 10% based on revenue tiers.
    - **Impact on Invoica**: Unlikely to apply unless Invoica engages in digital advertising revenue in Maryland. However, it sets a precedent for taxing digital services.
    - **Source**: Maryland Comptroller (comptroller.maryland.gov).
  - **New York**: Proposed a Digital Advertising Tax (A.4161/S.4102, 2023-2024 session), targeting companies with over $100 million in global revenue and $1 million in NY digital ad revenue. Rate: 2.5% to 10%. Not enacted as of October 2023.
    - **Impact on Invoica**: No current impact, but if passed, could indirectly affect digital platforms through increased costs for advertising partners.
    - **Source**: NY State Assembly (nyassembly.gov).
  - **Other States (CA, TX, FL, WA, IL, MA, NJ, PA, CO)**: No enacted DSTs as of 2023. Proposals in CA (e.g., AB 1780, data tax) and WA (digital ad tax discussions) have not progressed. TX, FL, IL, MA, NJ, PA, and CO have no active DST proposals.
    - **Impact on Invoica**: Minimal current impact, but future state DSTs could target digital payment or blockchain services.
    - **Source**: State legislative websites; MTC updates (mtc.gov).
